After nearly 20 years in B2B marketing — from enterprise brands like Oracle to high-growth companies like Neon One — I kept running into the same problem: GTM teams desperately need voice-of-customer intelligence, but the tools to get it are either too shallow, too slow, or too expensive.

Leaders need to understand the full buyer journey — the catalysts that started the search, the pain points driving urgency, how buyers evaluated options, and ultimately what tipped the decision. That depth of insight is transformative for product, sales, marketing, and support teams alike.

But the status quo doesn't work. Manual interviews are time-consuming and don't scale. Static surveys capture a checkbox, not a conversation — you learn a feature was missing, but not which feature. And the vendors who do deep buyer interviews? They charge upwards of $900 per survey, pricing out the majority of companies.

I kept thinking about how an AI-powered platform could solve this — one that could conduct personalized, adaptive interviews at scale, asking the smart follow-up questions that surface the real "why" behind every deal outcome. The kind of insight that helps teams actually improve and win more.

So I finally sat down and built it. As an AI-forward founder, I've built know-why.ai entirely using AI — and I believe it's the future of how every GTM team will understand their buyers.
